# NURBS scope decision ŌĆö general NURBS B-rep vs the bounded hybrid

**Status:** decision on the record (2026-07-10). **Verdict: do NOT build the general NURBS
B-rep core speculatively. The bounded analytic + freeform-hybrid approach is the correct scope
for the app as it exists; expand into NURBS only when a concrete freeform-surfacing feature on
the app roadmap forces it ŌĆö and then incrementally, demand-driven.**

This document exists because "OCCT has NURBS and we don't" recurs as a question. The honest
framing below separates the *facts* (what OCCT/Parasolid provide, what the reference apps use)
from the *decision* (what this kernel should invest in, and when).

---

## 1. Do the reference apps have general NURBS? ŌĆö Yes, foundationally

Every pro CAD tool the app is benchmarked against runs on a **commercial general NURBS B-rep
kernel**:

| App | Geometry kernel | NURBS B-rep? |
|---|---|---|
| **SolidWorks** | Siemens **Parasolid** | Ō£ģ full ŌĆö solids + the entire surfacing suite are NURBS |
| **Shapr3D** | Siemens **Parasolid** (licensed) | Ō£ģ full ŌĆö solids *and* surfaces are Parasolid NURBS B-rep |
| Onshape | Parasolid | Ō£ģ |
| Fusion 360 | Autodesk **ASM** (Parasolid lineage) | Ō£ģ |

So if the bar is *literal* parity, general NURBS is table stakes ŌĆö it is the underlying
representation that makes **arbitrary combinations of operations robust** and enables **freeform
surfacing**. CyberCadKernel deliberately chose a different point in the design space (below).

---

## 5. Decision

- **Do NOT take on the general NURBS core as one program.** It is the single largest possible
  scope item, and the app as it exists (mechanical/parametric per its measured `cc_*` usage) does
  not consume it.
- **The bounded pieces are the correct 80/20** ŌĆö bounded N-sided fill (Coons/Gregory patch over
  analytic boundaries ŌåÆ tessellated fill), bounded freeform booleans, curved blends ŌĆö deliver the
  practical freeform value at a tiny fraction of the cost, and are landed / landing demand-driven.
- **Trigger to reconsider:** if the app commits to genuine **freeform / organic / class-A
  surfacing** (the Shapr3D "arbitrary curves ŌåÆ smooth NURBS skin ŌåÆ tweak" workflow). Then build the
  NURBS layers **incrementally, driven by the specific surfacing features committed to** ŌĆö SSI and
  boolean first, as they gate the rest ŌĆö never speculatively.
- **CAE does not change this** ŌĆö it is served by meshing + accurate geometry + STEP exchange, all
  already present.

**One-line summary:** the reference apps have general NURBS (via Parasolid) and it is foundational
to them, but it is a ~20ŌĆō40 py investment this app does not need for mechanical/parametric CAD+CAE;
pursue it only if ŌĆö and incrementally as ŌĆö the product moves into freeform surfacing.
